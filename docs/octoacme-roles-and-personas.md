# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Risk Manager

### Role Summary
Risk Managers identify, assess, and monitor project risks to protect delivery timelines and outcomes. They coordinate risk response planning, maintain the risk register, and communicate critical risks to leadership, working closely with Project Managers to escalate issues as needed.

### Responsibilities
- Identify and assess project risks across technical, operational, and organizational dimensions
- Maintain and update the risk register with impact, probability, and mitigation plans
- Coordinate risk response strategies with Project Managers and relevant stakeholders
- Monitor active risks and trigger escalation protocols when thresholds are breached
- Communicate risk status and mitigation progress to project leadership

### Goals
- Minimize the impact of unplanned events on project delivery
- Ensure risks are surfaced early and assigned clear owners
- Foster a proactive risk culture across project teams

### Typical Communication
- Risk register updates shared with Project Managers and leadership
- Escalation reports for high-impact or high-probability risks
- Weekly risk review discussions during project syncs

---

## Stakeholder Engagement Lead

### Role Summary
Stakeholder Engagement Leads manage engagement strategies for both internal and external stakeholders. They ensure regular communication flows and feedback loops, and collaborate with Project Managers and communications teams to map stakeholder needs and manage expectations throughout the project lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Identify and map all relevant internal and external stakeholders
- Develop and maintain stakeholder engagement plans and communication calendars
- Facilitate feedback loops to capture stakeholder input at key project milestones
- Collaborate with Project Managers to align stakeholder expectations with project scope and timelines
- Escalate stakeholder concerns or conflicts to appropriate leads

### Goals
- Build and sustain positive relationships with all project stakeholders
- Ensure stakeholders are informed, engaged, and aligned with project objectives
- Reduce friction caused by miscommunication or unmanaged expectations

### Typical Communication
- Stakeholder mapping and engagement plans shared with Project Managers
- Regular stakeholder briefings and feedback session summaries
- Escalation notes when stakeholder concerns impact project direction

---

## Agile Coach

### Role Summary
Agile Coaches facilitate the adoption of Agile best practices and coach teams on frameworks such as Scrum or Kanban. They work closely with Product Owners, Project Managers, and delivery teams to identify process bottlenecks and drive continuous improvement.

### Responsibilities
- Guide teams in the adoption and consistent application of Agile frameworks (Scrum, Kanban, etc.)
- Facilitate retrospectives, sprint ceremonies, and team health checks
- Identify process inefficiencies and recommend improvements
- Coach Product Managers and Project Managers on Agile principles and servant leadership
- Support teams in self-organization and cross-functional collaboration

### Goals
- Increase team velocity and delivery predictability through sound Agile practices
- Build team capability to self-identify and resolve process issues
- Foster a culture of continuous improvement and psychological safety

### Typical Communication
- Retrospective action items and improvement plans shared with delivery teams
- Coaching sessions and workshops with Project Managers and Product Managers
- Process health dashboards and observations reported to leadership

---

## PMO Analyst

### Role Summary
PMO Analysts support the Project Management Office with reporting, resource planning, and standardization efforts. They collect project data, oversee metrics dashboards, and ensure process consistency across projects, communicating regularly with Project and Program Managers to harmonize practices.

### Responsibilities
- Collect, consolidate, and analyze project data across the portfolio
- Maintain and update metrics dashboards for leadership and project teams
- Support resource planning and capacity tracking across active projects
- Ensure standardized project management processes and templates are applied consistently
- Identify deviations from established processes and work with Project Managers to remediate

### Goals
- Provide accurate and timely portfolio-level visibility to leadership
- Standardize project management practices to reduce variability and improve outcomes
- Support data-informed decision-making across the PMO

### Typical Communication
- Portfolio status reports and metrics dashboards shared with leadership and Project Managers
- Process guidance and template updates distributed to delivery teams
- Resource planning summaries coordinated with Program and Project Managers
